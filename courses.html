OPERATIONS RESEARCH

Introduction

Operations research is a branch of science that makes use of analytical techniques in the fields of mathematical modeling, statistics and optimization helping students arrive at optimal solutions to complex decision making problems

Course Objective

A graduate in operations research is expected to be able to formulate mathematical, experimental and simulation models. They are expected to be able to define and evaluate operational problems and alternatively recommend computational and manipulative methods that will help solve these problems.

Course Duration

The program takes a total of 9 semesters with 2 semesters per academic year. Each student is required to undertake a practical attachment for a period of not less than 8 weeks at the end of the 8th semester. One may undertake an additional unit in their 2nd, 3rd and 4th year which does not count towards the classification of the degree but will appear on the transcript. One unit takes a series of 35 one lecture hours where a 3 hour practical period and a 2 hour tutorial period being equivalent to a 3 hour lecture.

Requirements

The following shall be eligible for admission considerations, into the programme:

1.Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) applicants with a mean aggregate of at least C+ and at least: 

  • C+ in both Mathematics and English OR
  • Credit in University Bridging Mathematics and C+ in English.
2. Kenya Advanced Certificate of Education (KACE) or A-level applicants with at least 2 principal passes, including Mathematics.
3. Holders of Diploma in Applied Sciences with at least a credit pass in relevant subjects from an institution recognized by the University Senate.
4. A holder of other qualifications recognized by the University Senate as equivalent to the 1 and 2 above.

Students with either qualification 1 or 3 may at the discretion of the Facuality of Science be considered for credit transfer.

Fees

For Kenyan citizens, tuition fees is Kshs.76,403.00 per semester in the first year, and ranges from Kshs. 57,303.00 to Kshs. 62,078.00 for all other semesters. For non citizens, add an extra 20% to the amount in every semester.
